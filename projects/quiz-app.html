QUIZ APP

Simple web app to built user knowledge in fine art movements from four major time periods

The problem - Art history is boring (sometimes).

‍

Brainstorming

‍

How can we make learning about art history more exciting and engaging for users? Gamification.

By adding a high score and easy user interface, its easy to encourage users to complete questions and lear while enjoying the experience. Our goal? To create an app that not only informs but inspires a deeper appreciation for art. We can complete the package by creating custom graphics that are art history inspired and bring the subject matter into the interface itself.

‍

Web application design

‍

The design of the app went through a few iterations before landing on the final product.

These initial designs were all done in Figma and were inspired by mobile space quiz apps an lifestyle tracking and improvement apps. The final color palette moved from a more natural browns and greens to a more bright and vibrant yellows and creams.

At one point the app was far more colorful and relied on the traditional three primary color scheme to play on the idea of art making in the interface. This was later scrapped because of a lack of cohesion between different pages of the app.

This stage was also where the idea of a mascot was introduced.

‍

Development & problem solving

‍

The app was developed using plain vanilla HTML, CSS and JavaScript, no frameworks. To reduce the amount of JavaScript, each question was generated from a single array of objects and passed through the same function.

Eventually however, I ran into the problem of how to save your score to encourage repeat plays.

Originally I used session storage that was increased by 1 every time a question was answered correctly. This was later changed to Local Storage after testing with users to address how users were discouraged when their score didn't save.

‍

‍

Graphics development

‍

To complete the package and bring the fine art concept full circle the project needed some art. I started by sketching on paper ideas of how to represent the four time periods. At this point I also conceptualized the mascot and additional graphics such as the trophy.

‍

It made sense to create all the graphics instead of just the main four so that the entire art direction of the project would be cohesive.

‍

Some of these concept went through multiple iterations before deciding on the final. Though there were concepts I really loved, some would not look good in the final size and space needed in the app.

After the concepts were designed, it was time to convert them to SVG's that were usable in the app. This process was entirely done in Figma. Each design was imported as a JPG and recreated using Figma to create high quality SVG's.  I prefer to work in SVG's for digital assets because of the ability to resize them without losing quality.  

‍

Deployment

‍

The project was launched to a live URL through GitHub and Netlify.

Overall this project was successful for a small quiz app, and accomplishes its initial goal of gamifying art history.

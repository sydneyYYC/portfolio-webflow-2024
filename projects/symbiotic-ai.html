Symbiotic AI Website

Building and rebranding the Symbiotic AI company website from the ground up with a modern sleek look.

Description of the project goals and the company needs.

‍

Symbiotic AI is a small tech startup in Calgary, AB. They needed a new website that showed off their company and product in development to potential investors. The site needed to represent who they are including their new branding and their flagship product. The total timeline of this project was three months.

‍

1. Initial Discovery

2. Web application design & optimization

3. Mobile application design & optimization

4. Development of Website in Next.14

5. Beta testing & final adjustments

6. Publishing & maintenance

‍

Initial Discovery– understanding the needs and goals of the project

‍

Symbiotic AI needed a website that told their story. My work began by asking the bigger questions, "what is the one thing you want people to get out of the site?". I was able to design a user experience that they felt captured their biggest ideas for their company: modern, technological and trustworthy. Our discussions lead into understanding and documenting their functional requirements, which I would use to decide on a tech stack that would fit their needs and budget.

‍

‍

Design

‍

The company had a single logo when I started on this project, and from that I was able to create a cohesive color palette that matched the current brand identity. Then I moved on to color and appropriate imagery, which became the design package for their new website.

I went through several iterations of the with the CEO and development team. Starting with five options of desktop layout designs we narrowed them down to three, then two. Eventually the team decided on their top pick, which I then expanded on into high fidelity wireframe using Figma.

‍

I designed a user experience that they felt captured the biggest ideas for their company: modern, technological and trustworthy.

‍

‍

Mobile Design

‍

Once we decided on a desktop design I began working on its mobile counterpart. Keeping in mind that this had to work on mobile screens which are much thinner then desktop, some aspects had to change.

‍

The navigation bar was replaced with a collapsible menu. This would maximize screen space and allow navigation links to remain large enough to be readable. The footer also went through major changes. The footer was changed from a horizontal spread out series of links to a vertical orientation. The goal for this change was to optimize the functionality of the footer navigation for using a thumb opposed to a mouse for integrations.

‍

‍

Development

‍

Halfway through the timeline of the project I began development. The project was built using Next.js 14 for its flexibility in both frontend and backend to handle all of the functionalities that the company needed. I used a Node backend to handle server requests and to send data from the site to the CEO directly without need for and further integrations.

‍

The site was styled using a custom Tailwind theme and is completely responsive for desktop, tablet and mobile devices.

The site is connected to a Google Analytics for monitoring user engagement. Further the site was checked by Digital Mainstreet and edited to improve SEO and with my efforts I brought the site to #1 on Google search.

After the development process was over I began testing the site using Azure Load Testing and a Virtual Machine to check for consistency across browsers and operating systems. These tests were thoroughly documented and analyzed to create a strategic plan for fixing errors before launch.

‍

Hosting and Maintenance

‍

After the development was finished, the site was hosted through Microsoft Azure and connected to the company's custom url. The final build was monitored and maintenance using Azure pipeline connected to a GitHub repository.

This ensures that any changes made to the site can be staged in a separate accessible place for approval before being published to the website.
